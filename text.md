**Study Objectives**  \n\n*Primary Objective*  \n- To evaluate the effect of **TrialDrug‑X 150 mg** administered intravenously every 3 weeks (q3 w) + standard trastuzumab + pertuzumab versus **matching placebo** + trastuzumab + pertuzumab on **overall survival (OS)** in adult patients with **HER2‑positive metastatic breast cancer (mBC).**  \n\n*Secondary Objectives*  \n\n1. **Progression‑free survival (PFS)** – assessed per RECIST v1.1.  \n2. **Objective response rate (ORR)** and **disease‑control rate (DCR)** – per RECIST v1.1.  \n3. **Duration of response (DoR)** and **time to deterioration of health‑related quality of life (HR‑QoL)** – using the EORTC QLQ‑C30 and QLQ‑BR23 instruments.  \n4. **Safety and tolerability** – incidence and severity of adverse events (AEs)/serious AEs (SAEs), laboratory abnormalities, cardiac safety (LVEF, QTcF), and treatment‑discontinuation rates.  \n\n*Exploratory Objectives*  \n\n- Pharmacokinetic (PK) profiling of TrialDrug‑X.  \n- Biomarker analyses (e.g., HER2‑expression level, circulating tumor‑DNA dynamics).  \n- Health‑economic outcomes (cost‑effectiveness, healthcare‑resource utilization).  \n\n---\n\n**Study Design**  \n\n| Attribute | Description |\n|-----------|-------------|\n| **Type** | Multicenter, international, randomized, double‑blind, placebo‑controlled, parallel‑group Phase III trial. |\n| **Randomization** | 1:1 allocation via a centralized interactive web‑response system (IWRS). Stratification factors: <br>  • Prior trastuzumab‑pertuzumab exposure (yes/no) <br>  • Disease‑free interval (<12 months vs ≥ 12 months) <br>  • Geographic region (North America, Europe, Asia‑Pacific). |\n| **Blinding** | TrialDrug‑X and matching placebo are double‑blinded; trastuzumab and pertuzumab are administered open‑label as standard of care. |\n| **Intervention Arm** | • TrialDrug‑X 150 mg IV infusion over 30 min q3 w  <br>• Trastuzumab 8 mg/kg loading dose, then 6 mg/kg q3 w  <br>• Pertuzumab 840 mg loading dose, then 420 mg q3 w |\n| **Control Arm** | • Matching placebo IV infusion over 30 min q3 w <br>• Identical trastuzumab + pertuzumab regimen (as above) |\n| **Treatment Duration** | Until documented disease progression, unacceptable toxicity, withdrawal of consent, or a maximum of 24 months of study‑drug exposure (whichever occurs first). |\n| **Follow‑up** | OS evaluated every 12 weeks after treatment discontinuation until 48 months post‑randomization or death, whichever occurs first. |\n| **Regulatory Framework** | Conducted in accordance with ICH E6(R2) GCP, ICH E8(R1), ICH E9(R1), FDA 21 CFR Part 312, and relevant EMA guidelines (including the Diversity and Endpoints reflection papers). |\n\n> **Figure 1.** *Study schema/flow diagram* (placeholder – to be inserted in the full protocol).  \n\n---\n\n**Study Population**  \n\n*Inclusion Criteria* – **All** must be satisfied  \n\n1. Adults ≥ 18 years at the time of informed consent.  \n2. Histologically confirmed **HER2‑positive** metastatic breast carcinoma (IHC 3+ or ISH‑positive per ASCO/CAP 2018).  \n3. At least one **measurable** lesion per RECIST v1.1.  \n4. ECOG performance status 0 or 1.  \n5. Adequate organ function: <br>  • ANC ≥ 1.5 × 10⁹/L <br>  • Platelets ≥ 100 × 10⁹/L <br>  • Hemoglobin ≥ 9 g/dL <br>  • Serum creatinine ≤ 1.5 × ULN **or** CrCl (Cockcroft‑Gault) ≥ 30 mL/min <br>  • AST/ALT ≤ 2.5 × ULN (≤ 5 × ULN if hepatic metastases) <br>  • Bilirubin ≤ 1.5 × ULN.  \n6. Left‑ventricular ejection fraction (LVEF) ≥ 55 % (echocardiogram or MUGA) within 28 days prior to randomization.  \n7. Baseline QTcF ≤ 470 ms (Fridericia correction).  \n8. Willingness to use **highly effective contraception** (≥ 2 methods) from screening until 6 months after the last dose (applicable to women of child‑bearing potential and male participants).  \n9. Signed informed‑consent form and agreement to comply with study procedures.  \n\n*Exclusion Criteria* – **Any** one will exclude the participant  \n\n1. Prior treatment with PI3K, AKT, or mTOR inhibitors.  \n2. Untreated or symptomatic CNS metastases; previously treated CNS disease must be stable for ≥ 8 weeks and off steroids (or on ≤ 10 mg prednisone‑equivalent).  \n3. Clinically significant cardiac disease (e.g., myocardial infarction, unstable angina, uncontrolled arrhythmia, NYHA class III/IV heart failure) within the past 6 months.  \n4. QTcF > 470 ms at screening.  \n5. Concurrent enrollment in another interventional trial or receipt of an investigational agent within 30 days (or 5 half‑lives) of randomization.  \n6. Known hypersensitivity to TrialDrug‑X, trastuzumab, pertuzumab, or any excipients.  \n7. Active infection requiring systemic therapy, including uncontrolled hepatitis B/C or HIV.  \n8. Pregnancy or breastfeeding.  \n\n---\n\n**Statistical Considerations**  \n\n- **Sample Size & Power** – 520 patients (≈ 260 per arm) will be randomized to accrue ~380 OS events, providing 80 % power to detect a hazard ratio (HR) of 0.75 (median OS 24 mo vs 18 mo) at a two‑sided α = 0.05.  \n\n- **Analysis Populations**  \n  - *Intent‑to‑Treat (ITT):* All randomized patients – primary efficacy set.  \n  - *Per‑Protocol (PP):* ITT patients without major protocol deviations – supportive analyses.  \n  - *Safety:* All patients receiving ≥ 1 dose of TrialDrug‑X or placebo.  \n\n- **Primary Efficacy Analysis**  \n  - OS compared using a **stratified log‑rank test** (stratification factors listed under Randomization).  \n  - HR and 95 % confidence interval (CI) estimated by a **Cox proportional‑hazards model**.  \n  - Kaplan‑Meier survival curves will be presented.  \n\n- **Secondary Endpoints**  \n  - **PFS:** Stratified log‑rank test, conditional on a statistically significant OS result (α = 0.01).  \n  - **ORR/DCR:** Proportions with exact 95 % CIs; between‑group comparison via chi‑square test.  \n  - **DoR:** Summarized descriptively; median and CI via Kaplan‑Meier.  \n  - **HR‑QoL:** Analyzed with a mixed‑effects model for repeated measures (MMRM), adjusting for baseline score and stratification factors.  \n\n- **Multiplicity Control** – Hierarchical testing sequence: OS → PFS → ORR/DCR → HR‑QoL. No additional statistical adjustment beyond this hierarchy.  \n\n- **Missing‑Data Handling**  \n  - Time‑to‑event outcomes censored at the last known alive/date.  \n  - HR‑QoL and PK variables: primary analysis uses available data; sensitivity analyses employ **multiple imputation** under a missing‑at‑random (MAR) assumption.  \n\n- **Interim Analyses**  \n  - One efficacy interim analysis after 50 % of OS events, applying O’Brien‑Fleming alpha‑spending to preserve the overall two‑sided α = 0.05.  \n  - An independent Data Monitoring Committee (DMC) will review safety data quarterly.  \n\n---\n\n**Assessment Schedule**  \n\n| Visit | Day(s) Relative to Randomization | Key Assessments |\n|-------|----------------------------------|-----------------|\n| **Screening** | –30 to –1 | Informed consent, eligibility labs, CBC/CMP, LFTs, renal panel, ECG, echocardiogram/MUGA, QTcF, tumor imaging (CT/MRI), HR‑QoL questionnaires, contraceptive counseling |\n| **Baseline (Day 1)** | 0 | Randomization, TrialDrug‑X/placebo infusion, trastuzumab & pertuzumab loading doses, pre‑dose PK sample, labs, ECG, LVEF |\n| **Treatment Cycle** | Day 1 of each 21‑day cycle | Study‑drug infusion, trastuzumab & pertuzumab maintenance doses, CBC/CMP, cardiac enzymes, AE/SAE assessment, ECG (every 6 weeks) |\n| **Tumor Assessment** | Every 9 weeks (±7 days) | CT/MRI per RECIST v1.1 |\n| **Cardiac Monitoring** | LVEF every 12 weeks;
